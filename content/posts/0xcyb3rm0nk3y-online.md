## Mainframe acquired.
For years I’ve ghosted through networks, popped boxes, collected TTPs, and watched the digital underground evolve. What started as curiosity has turned into a front-row seat to something bigger: AI reshaping the entire threat landscape and becoming an existential risk to how we live and work.  

Now it’s time to move from the shadows to the public grid. Welcome to my corner of it.My journey into this world began in the late ’90s with a dusty Pentium II machine that someone brought home. That clunky beige box became my escape. I spent countless hours immersed in Doom and Age of Empires, learning keyboard shortcuts before I fully understood what an operating system even was.

That same machine introduced me to the darker side of the early internet. The ILOVEYOU worm and Melissa virus were spreading like wildfire through email attachments. Adware was everywhere—especially if you were downloading from peer-to-peer networks like Napster or Limewire. I quickly learned that curiosity came with risks.Nothing captures that era quite like the screeching handshake of an AOL dial-up modem. That awful, beautiful noise still feels like home.
